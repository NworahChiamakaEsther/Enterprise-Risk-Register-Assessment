# Information Security Policy
StarLimited Inc
## 1. Purpose
The Information Security Policy defines the principles, responsibilities, and controls necessary to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization’s information assets.  
This policy ensures compliance with regulatory frameworks, protects against cybersecurity threats, and aligns with ISO/IEC 27001 best practices.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party partners
- All information systems, networks, and devices
- All organizational data, whether digital, physical, or intellectual property

---

## 3. Policy Statement
The organization is committed to:
- Protecting all sensitive, proprietary, and regulated information
- Maintaining secure access and authentication controls
- Detecting, preventing, and responding to cybersecurity threats
- Promoting a security-aware culture across all stakeholders

---

## 4. Information Security Principles
1. **Confidentiality:** Restrict access to authorized users only  
2. **Integrity:** Ensure data is accurate, complete, and protected from unauthorized modification  
3. **Availability:** Ensure reliable access to information when required  
4. **Accountability:** All users are responsible for their security activities

---

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|-----|----------------|
| Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) | Overall security governance, policy enforcement, incident response leadership |
| IT & Security Teams | Implement technical controls, monitor threats, conduct vulnerability assessments |
| Risk Owners | Ensure business processes comply with security requirements |
| Employees & Contractors | Adhere to security policies, report incidents, maintain secure practices |

---

## 6. Key Security Controls
- **Access Management:** Role-based access, MFA, least privilege principles  
- **Data Protection:** Encryption at rest and in transit, secure storage, classification of sensitive data  
- **Endpoint & Network Security:** Firewalls, EDR, intrusion detection, vulnerability scanning  
- **Incident Management:** Defined incident response plan, breach notification, and forensic investigation  
- **Physical Security:** Controlled access to data centers and offices, monitoring systems

---

## 7. Security Awareness and Training
- Annual security awareness training for all employees  
- Phishing simulations and cybersecurity drills  
- Continuous updates on emerging threats

---

## 8. Monitoring and Reporting
- Continuous monitoring of systems and networks  
- Periodic audits and compliance checks  
- Reporting incidents to management and regulators as required

---

## 9. Policy Compliance
Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to termination, and may involve legal consequences.

---

## 10. Review and Maintenance
- Annual review or after significant incidents  
- Updates based on changes in regulatory requirements or threat landscape  

---

## 11. References
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 – Information Security Management  
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework  
- NDPR, GDPR, and other applicable laws
