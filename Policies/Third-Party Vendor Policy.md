# Third-Party Vendor Risk Management Policy

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured approach for identifying, assessing, managing, and monitoring risks associated with third-party vendors.  
It ensures that vendor relationships support business objectives without exposing the organization to unacceptable operational, financial, compliance, or cybersecurity risks.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All vendors, suppliers, contractors, and service providers  
- All contracts involving access to organizational systems, data, or critical services  
- All business units engaging vendors in strategic, operational, or technical activities

---

## 3. Policy Statement
The organization is committed to:
- Ensuring all vendors are assessed for risk before engagement  
- Monitoring vendor performance and compliance throughout the contract lifecycle  
- Protecting sensitive information shared with vendors  
- Terminating or mitigating vendor relationships that pose unacceptable risk  

---

## 4. Vendor Risk Categories
- **Operational Risk:** Failure to deliver services or meet SLAs  
- **Cybersecurity Risk:** Unauthorized access, data breaches, malware  
- **Compliance Risk:** Regulatory or legal violations  
- **Financial Risk:** Insolvency or financial instability  
- **Reputational Risk:** Damage to the organization’s brand

---

## 5. Vendor Risk Management Lifecycle

### 5.1 Vendor Selection and Onboarding
- Conduct due diligence, including financial, operational, and compliance assessments  
- Review security certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001) and data protection practices  
- Classify vendors based on risk level

### 5.2 Contracting
- Include contractual clauses addressing:  
  - Data privacy and protection  
  - Security obligations  
  - Audit rights and reporting  
  - Business continuity and disaster recovery  
  - Termination and exit strategies

### 5.3 Continuous Monitoring
- Perform periodic risk reassessments  
- Track Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) such as access violations, SLA breaches, and audit findings  
- Escalate critical vendor risks to senior management

### 5.4 Offboarding
- Ensure secure data return or destruction  
- Revoke access to systems and facilities  
- Document lessons learned for future engagements

---

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|-----|----------------|
| Vendor Management Lead | Oversee vendor risk assessments and lifecycle management |
| Compliance Officer | Ensure regulatory and contractual compliance |
| IT & Security Teams | Assess vendor cybersecurity posture and monitor access |
| Business Owners | Identify operational needs and manage vendor performance |
| Risk Committee | Review high-risk vendors, approve risk mitigation actions |

---

## 7. Reporting and Escalation
- Monthly operational risk reports for vendor performance  
- Quarterly management risk reports for high-risk vendors  
- Annual board-level vendor risk summary  
- Immediate escalation of critical incidents or non-compliance

---

## 8. Policy Compliance
Non-compliance may result in termination of vendor contracts or internal disciplinary action for employees managing vendors.

---

## 9. Review and Maintenance
- Annual policy review  
- Updates triggered by regulatory changes, major incidents, or vendor risk events
